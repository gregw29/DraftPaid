Detailed Comparison

Availability & Geographic Restrictions

Wave: Limited to United States and Canada only. If you're a freelancer or business operating outside these countries, Wave is not an option.

DraftPaid: Available worldwide. Whether you're in the UK, Australia, EU, or anywhere else, DraftPaid works without restrictions. This is crucial for international freelancers and remote workers.

Signup & Account Requirements

Wave: Requires creating an account with email verification. Users must provide personal information and go through the onboarding process before creating their first invoice.

DraftPaid: No signup required. You can create a professional invoice in seconds without providing any personal information. Your invoice data is never stored on servers.

Payment Processing & Known Issues

Important: Wave has documented issues with payment processing holds that have caused serious problems for small business owners.

Wave: Offers payment processing through Wave Payments, but has a well-documented history of unexplained holds on transactions. According to Trustpilot reviews and BBB complaints, customers have reported:

  • Funds held for months without explanation
  • Automatic refunds issued to clients without merchant consent, destroying business relationships
  • Cases involving $50,000+ in held funds
  • Poor customer service response (41+ minute wait times reported)

DraftPaid: Uses Stripe for optional payment processing. Stripe is known for transparent hold policies and faster fund access. DraftPaid's primary focus is invoice generation, so payment processing is secondary.

Feature Scope & Complexity

Wave: Full accounting platform including invoicing, expense tracking, accounting, and payroll. This makes it feature-rich but also more complex. Some users report the interface overwhelming for simple invoicing needs.

DraftPaid: Focused solely on invoice generation. Intentionally simple and streamlined. If you need full accounting, Wave is more suitable. If you just need professional invoices quickly, DraftPaid is the better choice.

Cost Comparison

Wave: Free to use (invoicing), but charges for payment processing and payroll features. The payroll feature starts at $20-$40/month.

DraftPaid: Completely free for invoice generation. No hidden fees, no premium tiers, no forced upgrades.

Who Should Choose Each?

Choose Wave if:

  • You're based in the US or Canada
  • You need full accounting and expense tracking
  • You have employees and need payroll features
  • You want comprehensive financial management in one platform
  • You're willing to accept payment processing hold risks

Choose DraftPaid if:

  • You're located outside the US/Canada
  • You need to create invoices quickly without signup
  • You invoice irregularly or occasionally
  • You prefer simplicity over comprehensive features
  • Privacy and no data storage is important to you
  • You want a tool that "just works" without complexity

Final Verdict

Both DraftPaid and Wave serve different markets and use cases.

Wave is best for: US/Canada-based businesses that want a comprehensive accounting platform and don't mind the payment processing risks.

DraftPaid is best for: Freelancers and small businesses worldwide who need a quick, simple, free invoice generator without signup or data storage concerns.

If you're outside the US/Canada, DraftPaid is your only option. If you're in North America and want something simple, DraftPaid is faster and more straightforward. If you need full accounting, Wave provides more features despite its limitations.
